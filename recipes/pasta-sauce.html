Pasta Sauce

Description

A beautiful Italian pasta sauce that has its roots in Bari, Italy. This is a very rich, dark, and meaty sauce. Traditionally made with lamb necks, we can also substitute beef, pork, as well as various types of sausage. Using a cut or two of meat with some nice bones improves the flavor.

Our sauce is relatively simple, we will be using various canned tomato products instead of making it ourselves the old fashioned way. Cooking this sauce in a regular pot over the stove will need four or five hours to finish. Alternatively, if you have a pressure cooker, we can speed the cook time up to around 90 minutes. The pressure cooker also dramatically improves flavor in my opinion.

Ingredients

  • 2-3 pounds lamb necks (or your choice of meat)
  • Olive oil
  • 1 26 oz can tomato sauce
  • 1 26 oz can tomato puree
  • 1 26 oz can crushed tomatoes
  • 1 14 oz can tomato paste
  • 5-6 cloves garlic
  • 1 large onion
  • Salt and pepper

Steps

  1. Braise the meat for a minute each side in a pan with olive oil
  2. Simmer garlic in olive oil in a large pot (or pressure cooker) over medium heat
  3. Add a diced onion before the garlic begins to burn
  4. Add in all the cans of tomato sauces and pastes
  5. Add spices to taste
  6. Cook for 4 to 5 hours, stirring occasionally
  7. If using pressure cooker, only 90 minutes cooktime are needed once brought to pressure
