Peposo dell'Impruneta

Description

A very old Italian dish from pre-Columbian Tuscany, Peposo dell'Impruneta was made by Tuscan tile workers who heavily peppered their poor cuts of beef and sealed them in clay jars with red wine, left to cook in the hot sun for hours. Slow cooking in lots of wine, pepper, and herbs will turn any cut of meat into a delicacy!

Short ribs are recommended for this recipe, although just about any cut of beef with bones will work. You might think we are using too much pepper, but the bold presence of big pepper in combination with the subtle wine and herbs are what makes this dish unique.

Ingredients

  • 2 lbs beef short ribs, bone in
  • 2 tablespoons tomato paste
  • 2 cups dry red wine
  • 5-6 cloves garlic
  • 3-4 tablespoons peppercorns
  • 2 teaspoons ground pepper
  • 2 teaspoons salt
  • 6-7 sprigs of rosemary
  • 3-4 bay leaves
  • 5-6 sage leaves

Steps

  1. Grind the peppercorns
  2. Salt the short ribs generously on all sides
  3. Apply the ground peppercorns and ground pepper evenly to the short ribs
  4. Crush garlic into a paste and rub onto short ribs
  5. Smear tomato paste onto the short ribs
  6. Arrange short ribs in a large frying pan or dutch oven, and add the wine
  7. Throw in bay leaves, sage, and rosemary
  8. Bring the wine to a simmer and cover for 90 minutes
  9. After 90 minutes, turn the ribs every 30 minutes for another 3 or 4 hours
  10. When ribs are fork tender, remove bones and serve over pasta, polenta, or rice
